Marketing case study Imagined pitch

Porcelain Noir

A tribute collection by Maison Margiela Beauty, honoring the creative world of John Galliano and Pat McGrath presented at Paris Haute Couture Week, January 2026. A pitch built around a 25-year creative convergence.

00

Collaboration overview

The premise

A limited-edition beauty collection concept under Maison Margiela Beauty, built around the 25-year creative partnership between makeup artist Pat McGrath and designer John Galliano. The pitch frames the collection as a tribute — not a celebrity drop — anchored to Paris Haute Couture Week and a single, deliberately scarce release.

01

Brand strategy

Why not a celebrity collab?

"This brand doesn't sell 'fame'. It sells perspective and meaning."

The strategy opens on a deliberate question: why not a standalone product? Why not a celebrity collab? The answer is that Margiela Beauty × McGrath × Galliano is not a marketing arrangement — it is a convergence of three creative forces that have shaped fashion and beauty with lasting cultural impact.

Reason · 01

Structural credibility, not attention

Celebrity collabs bring short-term attention. But this convergence of three creative forces has shaped fashion and beauty with lasting cultural impact.

Not just marketing, but structural credibility.
Reason · 02

Long-term cultural value

Celebrity drops follow hype cycles. This collaboration elevates the collection into something with future cultural relevance.

Value that compounds over time, not tied to a moment or a person.
Reason · 03

Truth over visibility

Margiela has always communicated through concept, material, and art. This isn't entertainment — it's a meeting of creators the brand can stand beside without compromise.

Aligned with brand philosophy, not in conflict with it.
02

Product design

The object

The collection translates the partnership into a physical object — a tribute housed across two drawers within one Victorian runway house, drawing from Galliano's own-label FW07 Elizabethan Wonderland and Maison Margiela SS24 Artisanal. The product is engineered to generate its own viral content without a campaign brief: the Invisible Mask technique becomes the hero mechanic of the design.

03

Market positioning & competitive analysis

Where Porcelain Noir sits

Why Porcelain Noir has no direct competitors.

Three collabs each captured one layer of what Porcelain Noir combines — but none achieved all three simultaneously. Each one gets part of the equation right, but not all of it. "Porcelain Noir" is the only one where the distribution is restrictive enough to function as a collector mechanic and the cultural legitimacy existed before the campaign did.

Possible competitor · 01

Valentino Beauty

Pierpaolo Piccioli × Raoul Alejandre × Valentino

Art-directed and credible, but it sells through Sephora and global retail — accessible by design, not exclusive.

Possible competitor · 02

Half Magic

Donni Davy × A24 × Euphoria series

Had genuine organic demand from Euphoria, but the brand itself is a commercial response to that moment, not the moment itself — and it distributes through Ulta and Sephora.

Possible competitor · 03

Byredo Cosmetics

Byredo × Isamaya Ffrench

Comes closest in terms of conceptual restraint, but the partnership was built for the collab. There was no pre-existing relationship the audience already knew.

Why the product can't be replicated. Byredo proves the makeup-artist co-creator model works. Half Magic proves demand from existing creative work converts to product sales. Valentino proves a fashion house can anchor a beauty line without celebrity. However, none of them do all three simultaneously — and none have a product that generates its own viral content without a campaign brief.

Map is directional, not data-driven. Based on brand positioning analysis and press coverage.
04

Target audience & study demographics

Who it's for

The collection is positioned for a collector-minded audience that recognizes cultural legitimacy before a campaign tells them to — the segment that follows McGrath and Galliano's authorship directly, and treats a scarce, numbered release as identity rather than purchase.

05

Marketing strategy & PR campaign

Awareness → sold out in 6 hours

How awareness becomes a sold-out in six hours.

Every phase of this campaign maps to a specific job. Three platforms, three rhythms — each with a different job in the funnel — converging on a single midnight drop with no restock.

TikTok

The Invisible Mask face reveal is the content itself, with Pat McGrath posting first and the community instantly recreating it — removing the need for any creator brief as the product naturally generates its own campaign.

Target: 2M organic impressions · Zero paid spend
Comparable: SS24 show hit 50M+ with no paid campaign at all. (WWD, Jan 2024)
Instagram

McGrath posts the first Invisible Mask activation. The face appearing on camera is the content. 50 Runway house press kits land simultaneously.

Target: 500K pre-interest registrations
TikTok beauty content: 100B+ views in 2023. Recurring SS24 recreation video alone: 2.1M likes.
Xiaohongshu

The skincare sequence unfolds through serum selection, brush activation, and mask application — turning multi-step routines into compelling content, while collector-driven posts like "which face did you get" amplify engagement and deepen the sense of ownership and identity within the drop.

Target: 500K reach · 5K saves on ritual posts
Xiaohongshu luxury beauty: 2.3B posts in 2023. K-beauty skincare ritual content drives highest save rates.
YouTube

A 90-second hero campaign film unfolds as a mini-documentary tracing 25 years of the collab, with McGrath herself explaining the Invisible Mask technique — grounding the story in authorship while elevating it beyond a typical campaign into a cultural and archival piece.

Target: 2M views · 80% completion rate on hero film
Comparable: Byredo campaign film: 1.8M views organic. Documentary-style beauty content: avg 78% completion. (YouTube data, 2024)
Private reveal + Gwendoline Christie

80 guests at the Paris opening event. McGrath demonstrates the mask live. Christie, who closed the SS24 show, inspired this collection.

Target: every major fashion and beauty editor in the room before launch
Christie closed the Margiela SS24 Artisanal show, January 2024.
Social media · Physical drop

3,000 units with no restock. Launch at midnight Paris time. One unit per customer. No retail partners. The sold-out timestamp becomes part of the story. The resale market begins within hours.

Target: sold out in under 6 hours
McGrath early drops sold out within minutes — her own words. (Fashionista, Aug 2017)
05·b

Content calendar

Three platforms, three rhythms
Phase 01Nov 2025 · Wk 1–2

All three accounts go dark. Profile images change to a single white mask. No announcement, no explanation. Fashion press notices within 48 hours. Zero paid spend.

Phase 02Nov 2025 · Wk 3 – Dec

The Invisible Mask face reveal is the content itself — Pat McGrath posting first and the community instantly recreating it, removing the need for any creator brief as the product naturally generates its own campaign.

Phase 03Nov Wk 4 – 13 Dec

One Invisible Mask model face revealed per week for ten weeks. Pre-interest list opens. Community starts discussing, tracking, and anticipating which face they want.

Phase 04Wk 14 · Jan 19

Eighty-guest event in Paris. McGrath demonstrates the Invisible Mask live. The face appears in real time. Demonstration is filmed but no posting until the promised date.

Phase 05Wk 15 · Jan 20

Pre-order opens at midnight Paris time. 3,000 units across maisonmargiela.com, patmcgrath.com, and four flagships. Hero film drops. Target: sold out in under 6 hours.

Phase 06Wk 16 · Jan 30

Global press drops in sync with launch — System Magazine exclusive; Vogue / Dazed / i-D / AnOther features; Allure technical story; WWD + Business of Fashion industry coverage.

Phase 07Wk 17–18 · Jan–Feb

Margiela Artisanal show. McGrath posts backstage content referencing Porcelain Noir explicitly for the first time. Second wave of press.

Phase 08Wk 19+ · Feb–Mar

300-unit Archive Edition via blind lottery. All products plus a numbered art print, signed by McGrath. €280. Never restocked.
