One video, one still, double the reach

The pattern

Across two very different brand collaborations — a Thai contact lens brand and a MAC PR package — I found myself using the same two-part posting structure. It was not planned as a system at first. It came from noticing that a single video or a single carousel was rarely enough to fully extend the reach of a product collaboration.

The structure became clear over time. The first post was a video focused on product use and visual impact, designed to catch attention quickly and perform well through reach. The second was a still carousel inspired by a K-pop idol reference, giving audiences something more detailed to save, revisit, and share as a makeup reference. Together, the two formats helped extend the lifespan of the collaboration beyond a single upload.

Part 01 — Video

Product in use, filmed for reach

The video format focused on showing the product in use through close-up shots, movement, and editorial-style editing. Its role was reach, using the Reel format to push the content toward non-followers who may not already know the brand.

Part 02 — Still carousel

Idol reference, designed to be saved

The carousel paired the product with a K-pop idol whose aesthetic matched the overall look and mood of the content. It worked both as a makeup reference and a cultural hook — making the post more searchable, save-able, and likely to reach audiences already connected to the idol's fanbase.

EX 01

Contact lenses are invisible and the look has to do the work

Glitzlens + Minnie (G·I-DLE)

Glitzlens creates silicone hydrogel contact lenses, but lenses are naturally difficult to capture on camera without an extreme close-up. That meant the content had to rely more on styling, mood, and character than the product itself.

Minnie became the reference point because her image closely matches Glitzlens' audience: playful, feminine, and visually bold. Bubble overlays and spherical candle props were also used throughout the video to reflect the soft iridescent quality of the lenses on screen.

EX 02

PR package received. Campaign thinking applied.

MAC Powder Kiss × Ella (NewJeans)

MAC Cosmetics Thailand sent a Powder Kiss PR package with five lipstick shades, so I built the concept around a Valentine's date night look using pink heart ears, lace styling, and soft feminine visuals. The video focused on swatches, application shots, and showcasing all five shades within the same romantic world.

The second post took a more strategic direction. Ella Gross is the lead ambassador for MAC Powder Kiss in Thailand, so the carousel combined my own polaroid-style photos with Ella's campaign imagery to connect the content to an audience already engaging with the campaign online.
