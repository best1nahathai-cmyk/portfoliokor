The making of storyboard

Pre-production

The video opens with all characters introducing themselves one by one, each with a distinct look and energy. It then transitions into the clock sequence, where the perfume bottles dissolve into a close face and take position around it, with the moving heart symbolising its day longevity without needing any text on screen. From there, the video shifts into a product showcase featuring voiceover, close-up details, and real-use footage. The ending returns to a more direct and grounded tone with product information, the perfume being slipped into a bag, and the character walking out of frame.

Three parts, one story arc

Structure

Part 01 · Open

Open with each character introducing herself

Don't open with the bottle. Open with the woman. Each character is shown for under 2s with their archetype caption — "Girl Don't Cry," "Born To Love." This gave the post a flick-and-stop frame and a different visual style for each scent.

Part 02 · Concept

Visual metaphor for scent longevity

The four perfume bottles arrange around a face like a clock. The moving heart hand inside represents the day-long character of each scent. The visual exists without any need for a caption explaining day longevity.

Part 03 · Pact shot

Depth and editorial pact shot

An up-close macro pour into the bottle, placed at the centre of the frame, surrounded by close-up textures and ad shots. The hint of full ritual: ground crystals, scent fade, a final pull-out to the character walking out of the frame.

The content was divided into three distinct sections, each with its own visual direction and purpose. Before filming, I mapped out how each part would flow into the next and decided the transitions were not connecting as smoothly as they should. That became something I reworked during pre-production before the shoot began.

01

No brief was given, I wrote one myself

The brief I wrote

Bad Bitch Club is a Thai perfume brand with four distinct scents, each named after a different female archetype: Bitch Bitch, Girl Don't Cry, Born To Love, and #YOLO. The brand creators would have posted a simple product shot with a caption and left it there.

I saw the structure for a campaign instead. Four scents meant four characters, four aesthetics, and the opportunity to turn the collection into a full content series. So I wrote the brief myself: build a distinct look for each character, cast all four personalities visually, and produce the project in two different formats.

02

Study the look before building it

Look study

The brand names each scent after a different archetype, so I treated every one as a fully developed character rather than just a mood or aesthetic. Each look was built with its own energy, styling, makeup direction, and no specific content request from the brand. Most creators would have posted a simple product shot with a caption and left it there.

03

Outfit match the scent's personality

Outfit match

Outfit selection was never just about what looked good on camera. Each look had to feel believable for the character wearing it. The contrast between all four characters needed to be instantly clear within a single frame, so even without labels, viewers could immediately sense that each one had a different personality and energy.

Scent · BITCH BITCH

Bitch Bitch

A stylish leather coat, layered black on black on black. Confidence is the perfume's primary note — heavy oud, pepper, dry vanilla. Stand inside the doorway, do not enter.

Scent · Girl Don't Cry

Girl Don't Cry

Sheer silk slip dress in cream. Soft confidence — pear, jasmine, white musk. The girl who never raises her voice and still gets what she wants.

Scent · #YOLO

#YOLO

Cargo, hoop earrings, statement sneakers. Smoke, gin, mandarin and a sharp metallic tail. Always says yes, regrets nothing in the morning.

Scent · Born To Love

Born To Love

A pink heart, blush lace gloves and soft sweater. Vanilla, milky peach, neroli — for someone who falls in love with the city before its people.

06

Building everything from scratch

Production
Set build · BTS · 14:02
Production checklist
  • Source four character wardrobes. One scent = one silhouette, no overlap allowed.
  • Build clock orbit rig. Four bottles, one moving heart, hand-controlled rotation.
  • Cast and choreograph the four character entrances inside a single 12-second arc.
  • Macro lens + pump bottle for the pour shot. Surface gloss + cold ambient light only.
  • Cut twice — full video for IG Reel, still carousel for the discovery format.

The concept was clear from the beginning, but bringing it to life meant building everything from scratch. After finalising the storyboard and locking in the set, props, and stack points based on the brand's visual identity, every prop and set element was carefully sourced to match the original vision. The process always started with the storyboard first, with the set designed to support the concept rather than shape it afterwards.

07

One concept executed in two formats

Outcome

The concept was produced in two formats: a video and a still carousel. The video introduced all four characters in sequence and reached 49K views, while the carousel gave each character her own dedicated frame and reached 13K. The still format performed better because the concept benefits from slower viewing — and more visual detail. Giving each character space to stand on her own made the overall idea feel more immersive and memorable.
