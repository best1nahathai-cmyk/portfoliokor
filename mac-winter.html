Pre-production

The set was sourced and dressed before camera rolled. Snow textures, ornaments, reindeer figurines, ice linen, sheer pearl-trim gloves — every element had a place inside a single overarching mood. By the time talent arrived, the world they were stepping into already existed.

01

One sentence forms the narrative

The Brief

The MACster competition brief came with just one requirement: create content featuring the phrase "I only wear MAC." There was no theme, aesthetic direction, or campaign concept attached.

The easiest approach would have been filming a makeup tutorial, saying the line once, and posting. Instead, I approached it like a campaign question — if MAC were releasing a winter collection, what kind of world would surround it? That became the creative direction I built for the project myself.

Voice over · open

"This isn't a tutorial. This is a winter I only wear MAC."

Establishing line, voiced low-key against the cold-blue overhead. Read once at 00:00:04, never repeated.
Treatment beat

"A full winter — props, talent, ornaments, lighting — all in the same icy palette. The phrase carries the line. The look carries the story."

From the treatment doc: each product becomes the centre of its own moment, never the subject of a tip or trick.

03

The look had to feel like winter

Direction

The makeup direction was designed to feel seasonal and editorial rather than like a typical GRWM video. That is the main reason why I picked blue makeup. I used icy tones on the eyes to reflect the Sugar Crystal packaging, while the Studio Fix powder created a soft matte base that pre-empted catching the light naturally, and the Matte Satin lipstick in a cool nude shade kept the overall look balanced without overpowering the set design.

The winter set didn't exist before this project. Every detail had to be sourced, prepared, and arranged from scratch before filming began. From the ornaments and ambient props to the reindeer Christmas linens, snow textures, fabrics, and lighting setup — each element was chosen to stay within the icy-blue, grey, white, black, and gold colour palette inspired by the collection packaging, so the entire set would feel visually cohesive on camera.
